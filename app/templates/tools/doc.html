Fraud Detection Features Documentation

Income

Description: The declared income of the customer.

Importance: Income level can help identify abnormal behaviors, such as spending disproportionate to income.

Name Email Similarity

Description: A score representing the similarity between the customer's name and the email address used.

Importance: Low similarity might indicate the use of a fraudulent email address.

Previous Address Months Count

Description: The number of months the customer has lived at their previous address.

Importance: Customers who frequently change addresses may present a higher risk of fraud.

Current Address Months Count

Description: The number of months the customer has lived at their current address.

Importance: A short period at the same address could be an indicator of fraud, especially when combined with other suspicious factors.

Customer Age

Description: The age of the customer.

Importance: Certain age groups may be more vulnerable to fraud or more likely to be involved in fraudulent activities.

Days Since Request

Description: The number of days since the request was submitted.

Importance: Recent or very old requests may present different risks depending on the context.

Intended Balance Amount

Description: The amount the customer intends to maintain in their account.

Importance: An unusual or high amount compared to the customer's usual habits may be suspicious.

Payment Type

Description: The type of payment method used (credit card, wire transfer, etc.).

Importance: Certain types of payments may be more often associated with fraud.

ZIP Count (Last 4 Weeks)

Description: The number of ZIP codes associated with the customer's transactions in the last 4 weeks.

Importance: A large number of different ZIP codes might indicate fraudulent behavior, particularly in cases of identity theft.

Velocity (Last 6 Hours)

Description: The number of transactions made by the customer in the last 6 hours.

Importance: A high frequency of transactions in a short period might be a sign of fraud.

Velocity (Last 24 Hours)

Description: The number of transactions made by the customer in the last 24 hours.

Importance: Helps identify abnormal or unusual transactional behaviors.

Velocity (Last 4 Weeks)

Description: The total number of transactions made by the customer in the last 4 weeks.

Importance: Helps detect unusual transactional patterns over a longer period.

Bank Branch Count (Last 8 Weeks)

Description: The number of bank branches used by the customer in the last 8 weeks.

Importance: Frequent use of multiple branches can be an indicator of fraud.

Employment Status

Description: The current employment status of the customer (employed, unemployed, retired, etc.).

Importance: Employment status can affect the likelihood of fraud, especially in case of mismatch with other information.

Credit Risk Score

Description: A score representing the customer's credit risk.

Importance: A low score may indicate a higher likelihood of fraud.

Email Is Free

Description: Indicates whether the customer's email address is associated with a free service (e.g., Gmail, Yahoo).

Importance: Free emails are easier to obtain anonymously and can be an indicator of fraud.

Housing Status

Description: The customer's housing status (owner, renter, etc.).

Importance: Housing status can provide clues about the customer's financial stability.

Phone Home Valid

Description: Indicates whether the customer's home phone number is valid.

Importance: An invalid phone number may be an indicator of fraud or a false identity.

Phone Mobile Valid

Description: Indicates whether the customer's mobile phone number is valid.

Importance: An invalid mobile phone number may be an indicator of fraud or a false identity.

Bank Months Count

Description: The number of months the customer has held a bank account.

Importance: A very short duration may be suspicious, especially if other fraud indicators are present.

Has Other Cards

Description: Indicates whether the customer has other credit or debit cards.

Importance: Having multiple cards can increase the risk of fraud.

Proposed Credit Limit

Description: The amount of credit limit proposed for the customer.

Importance: A high credit request relative to the customer's usual profile may signal fraudulent behavior.

Foreign Request

Description: Indicates whether the request was made from a foreign country.

Importance: Requests from foreign countries may be more susceptible to fraud, especially if they do not match the customer's profile.

Source

Description: The channel through which the request was made (web, mobile, etc.).

Importance: Some channels may be more vulnerable to fraudulent activities.

Session Length (Minutes)

Description: The length of the session in minutes during the request.

Importance: An unusually long or short session duration may indicate a fraud attempt.

Device OS

Description: The operating system of the device used to make the request.

Importance: Knowing the operating system can help identify suspicious behaviors, such as detecting an OS rarely used by the customer.

Keep Alive Session

Description: Indicates whether the user's session is kept alive over an extended period.

Importance: A session kept active without action might be a sign of malicious manipulation.

Device Fraud Count

Description: The number of frauds previously associated with the device used.

Importance: A device with a history of frauds is a high-risk indicator.

Month

Description: The month during which the request was made.

Importance: Certain times of the year may see an increase in fraudulent activities, making this an important factor to monitor.
